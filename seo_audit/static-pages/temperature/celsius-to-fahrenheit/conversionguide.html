The Core Conversion

The Celsius scale (°C) is used internationally, while Fahrenheit (°F) is common in the U.S. Conversion ensures accurate temperature interpretation.

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

Example:

25 °C → (25 × 9/5) + 32 ≈ 77 °F

Sometimes you need to go the other way. Our Fahrenheit to Celsius converter helps you quickly convert °F to °C online. Perfect for recipes, scientific work, or interpreting international weather reports.

The Core Conversion

Fahrenheit (°F) is primarily used in the United States, while Celsius (°C) is used internationally. Use this formula for accurate conversions:

°C = (°F − 32) × 5/9

Example:

77 °F → (77 − 32) × 5/9 ≈ 25 °C
