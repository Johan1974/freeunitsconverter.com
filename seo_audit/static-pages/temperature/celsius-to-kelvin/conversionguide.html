The Core Conversion

The Celsius scale (°C) is widely used internationally, while the Kelvin scale (K) is the standard in scientific contexts, particularly in physics and chemistry. Converting between these scales ensures accurate temperature measurement and consistent data interpretation.

K = °C + 273.15

Example:

25 °C → 25 + 273.15 = 298.15 K

Kelvin to Celsius Converter

Sometimes you need to convert back from Kelvin to Celsius. Our Kelvin to Celsius converter allows quick and accurate conversions, which is particularly useful for lab work, physics calculations, or interpreting scientific data.

The Core Conversion

The Kelvin scale is an absolute temperature scale starting at absolute zero, while Celsius is a relative scale based on the freezing and boiling points of water. Use this formula to convert Kelvin back to Celsius:

°C = K − 273.15

Example:

298.15 K → 298.15 − 273.15 = 25 °C
