The Core Conversion

The Fahrenheit scale (°F) is primarily used in the United States, while Kelvin (K) is the standard in scientific contexts, particularly in physics and chemistry. Converting Fahrenheit to Kelvin ensures accurate temperature measurement and consistent data interpretation.

K = (°F − 32) × 5/9 + 273.15

Example:

77 °F → (77 − 32) × 5/9 + 273.15 ≈ 298.15 K

The Core Conversion

The Kelvin scale is an absolute temperature scale starting at absolute zero, while Fahrenheit is commonly used in the United States for weather and daily temperature measurements. Use this formula to convert Kelvin back to Fahrenheit:

°F = (K − 273.15) × 9/5 + 32

Example:

298.15 K → (298.15 − 273.15) × 9/5 + 32 ≈ 77 °F
